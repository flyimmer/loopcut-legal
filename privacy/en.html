LoopCut is a digital well-being app that helps you reduce unconscious phone scrolling and build more intentional phone habits. LoopCut is designed so sensitive phone-use information stays on your device whenever possible.

1. Who We Are

This Privacy Policy explains how LoopCut collects, uses, stores, and protects information when you use the LoopCut Android app and related services.

The data controller is Wei Zhang, Germany. You can contact us at u9486010837@gmail.com.

2. Short Summary

  • Most LoopCut settings and phone-use data are stored locally on your device.
  • LoopCut does not sell your data.
  • LoopCut does not use advertising identifiers or ad tracking.
  • LoopCut does not send app names, package names, websites, URLs, typed intentions, notification content, messages, browsing history, foreground app history, or personal content for reliability reports or product analytics.
  • Reliability reports help us find setup failures, crashes, and technical problems. You can control this in Settings.
  • Product analytics is optional and consent-based. Firebase Analytics events are sent only after you consent.
  • Support diagnostics are sent only when you choose to send a diagnostic report.

4. Account and Sign-In Data

You can use LoopCut without signing in unless a specific Early Access, premium, or account-based feature requires sign-in.

If you sign in with Google, LoopCut may process:

  • Google account email address for sign-in, account access, and account recovery.
  • Google account identifier to connect your LoopCut account to your Google sign-in.
  • Display name, if provided by Google, for account display or personalization.
  • Account status, such as Early Access, free, registered, premium, or expired.
  • Sign-in and security records to keep your account secure and prevent abuse.

Signing out disconnects your account from the app on that device. Signing out does not automatically delete your local app settings, and it is separate from your reliability-report and product-analytics choices.

5. Premium, Purchase, and Entitlement Data

If LoopCut offers paid or premium features, LoopCut may process purchase and entitlement data needed to provide those features, validate purchases, restore purchases, handle refunds, and comply with legal obligations.

  • Product or subscription identifier.
  • Purchase token or receipt information needed for validation.
  • Subscription, trial, renewal, expiration, cancellation, refund, or restore-purchase status.
  • Payment, tax, invoice, accounting, dispute, or legal records where required.

Purchase and entitlement records are service-operation data. They are not Firebase product analytics events.

6. Reliability Reports

LoopCut may collect limited reliability data to find setup failures, crashes, and technical issues across Android versions and phone brands. Reliability reports help us keep LoopCut working on difficult Android devices.

This may include:

  • App version and build number.
  • Android major version.
  • Coarse device or manufacturer bucket, such as Samsung, Xiaomi, Vivo, Honor, Huawei, or Other.
  • Permission and setup state, such as enabled, disabled, not supported, or unknown.
  • Failed setup step or setup failure category.
  • Crash, error, or reliability category.
  • Limited technical diagnostics such as error code category and affected app component.

Reliability reports do not include app names, package names, websites, URLs, typed intentions, notification content, messages, browsing history, foreground app history, or personal content.

You can turn reliability reports on or off in Settings - Privacy & Data.

Turning reliability reports off stops future reliability-report uploads. It does not delete account, purchase, legal, security, or support records that LoopCut needs to keep for other purposes.

7. Optional Product Analytics

Product analytics is optional and based on your consent. If you choose to help improve LoopCut during setup or enable product analytics in Settings, LoopCut may collect limited product usage events to understand where users get stuck and which features are useful.

Product analytics may include:

  • Onboarding completion and setup drop-off events.
  • Permission setup funnel events.
  • Activation and app-configuration buckets.
  • Premium screen and purchase funnel events.
  • Sleep Guard adoption and configuration buckets.
  • Language distribution.
  • App version, Android version, and coarse device or manufacturer bucket.

Product analytics does not include app names, package names, websites, URLs, typed intentions, notification content, messages, browsing history, foreground app history, or personal content.

LoopCut does not send a custom anonymous user ID for product analytics. Firebase / Google Analytics for Firebase may use built-in pseudonymous identifiers, such as an app-instance identifier or user_pseudo_id.

Before Consent

Before you consent to product analytics, Firebase Analytics remains disabled. LoopCut does not send remote product analytics events before consent, and LoopCut does not keep a raw pre-consent product analytics event queue for later upload. The app may keep coarse local counters or snapshots only for local app behavior.

Withdrawal

You can withdraw product analytics consent at any time in Settings - Privacy & Data. Turning it off stops future product analytics collection and clears local analytics summaries. It does not delete product settings, account state, premium entitlement state, purchase or legal records, or necessary service-operation records.

8. Member Updates and Offers

If you choose to receive member updates or exclusive offers, LoopCut may use your email address to send Early Access updates, product news, tips, or offers. This is optional and based on your consent.

You can unsubscribe at any time using the unsubscribe link in the email or by contacting us.

9. Service-Operation Data

Some data is processed separately from Firebase product analytics because it is necessary for app, account, payment, security, support, or legal operation. This may include:

  • Account creation, login, and account recovery.
  • Premium entitlement state.
  • Google Play purchase validation, subscription status, and restore-purchase processing.
  • Payment, tax, refund, accounting, dispute, and legal records where applicable.
  • Security, fraud-prevention, abuse-prevention, and essential server or API logs.
  • User-initiated support diagnostics.

Service-operation data is not used as Firebase product analytics unless the same interaction is separately collected as a consented product analytics event.

10. Support and Diagnostic Reports

If you contact support or choose to send a diagnostic report, LoopCut may receive technical details that help investigate hard-to-reproduce problems. Diagnostic reports are user-initiated and should be reviewable before sending where the app provides that flow.

A diagnostic report may include:

  • App version and build number.
  • Android version and coarse device or manufacturer bucket.
  • App language.
  • Relevant permission status.
  • Relevant error logs or reliability categories.
  • Account or entitlement status if needed to solve an account or premium issue.

By default, diagnostic reports are designed not to include app names, package names, typed intentions, notification content, screen text, websites, URLs, browsing history, or foreground app history. If you add screenshots, messages, or free-text descriptions to a support request, avoid including sensitive information unless it is necessary for support.

12. Cloud Synchronization

Signing in with Google is used to unlock account-based, Early Access, or premium functionality where those features are available. LoopCut does not sync your guarded app list, app usage history, intervention history, typed intentions, or Sleep Guard schedule to our servers by default.

If LoopCut adds optional cloud synchronization in the future, LoopCut will explain what data is synced and provide a separate control before syncing private settings or history.

13. Permissions and Device Access

LoopCut may ask for Android permissions or device settings access so the app can provide the features you choose to use. The exact permission names and setup steps may vary by Android version and phone manufacturer.

LoopCut uses these permissions for the app's digital well-being functions, local reminders, local device checks, and reliable operation. Permission-related data is processed on your device by default unless this policy explains a specific consented or necessary transmission.

LoopCut does not use permissions to collect screen content, messages, passwords, notification content, browsing history, typed intentions, or personal content for analytics. You can change or disable Android permissions in your device settings.

14. Accessibility Service

LoopCut uses Android's Accessibility Service only for digital well-being and app-intervention features. It helps LoopCut apply the protection settings you choose in the app.

LoopCut does not use the Accessibility Service to read screen content, text, UI labels, messages, passwords, or typed input. LoopCut does not record or transmit what you do inside other apps.

You can disable the Accessibility Service at any time in Android Settings.

17. Data Retention and Deletion

  • Local data is stored on your device and is normally deleted when you uninstall LoopCut, unless your Android or device backup settings keep a separate copy.
  • Account data is kept while your account is active and can be deleted in the app if account deletion is available, or by contacting us.
  • Purchase and legal records may be kept as long as required for accounting, tax, refund, fraud prevention, dispute handling, or legal obligations.
  • Reliability reports are kept only as long as needed to diagnose crashes, setup failures, and reliability issues.
  • Consented product analytics raw event-level data exported to BigQuery is retained for 90 days.
  • Longer-term analytics tables are aggregate-only and are designed not to contain user_pseudo_id, install IDs, package names, intention text, or event-level timelines.
  • Support diagnostics are kept only as long as needed to handle your support request.
